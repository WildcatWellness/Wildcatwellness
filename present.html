Be Present

Use reflection to recognize your inner thoughts and feelings. Be curi-ous and non- judgmental and give yourself permission to feel the way you do. Although some avoiding can be useful, “stuffing” or ignoring strong emotions can have negative impacts.

Meditation can be a useful tool to help us stay present in the moment. Click on these links below to get started!

Consider practicing Self - Compassion

As you work through challenging or uncertain times. Try some of these exercises compiled by Dr. Kristin Neff, a renowned researcher of self-compassion.

“Digital Breaks” can be useful and allow you to Be Present in the here and now. During these breaks take time to focus on something enjoyable. Go for a walk or a run and get some fresh air. Engage in a hobby that you enjoy. Do something to distract yourself from all the noise around you. Participating in activities that nourish your mind, body and soul is good self-care.

Grounding

Grounding strategies can help us get in touch with our bodies and surroundings in the present moment. Pause what you are doing and pay attention to what you can see, hear, smell, and touch in your environment. The self-soothing techniques below will help keep you grounded.

Be present in your conversations with friends, family, classmates, and others. It is okay to talk about difficult subjects; such as politics, racial inequality, COVID-19, or anything else that feels difficult for you. Use “I” statements to convey your thoughts and feelings. Be curious about others opinions and be confident in yours. If you are unsure about something someone says, clarify, do not make assumptions. Acknowledge your understanding of what others say, even if you disagree. Acknowledgement does not mean agreement! And, most importantly, know when to end the conversation and how to set your boundaries.
