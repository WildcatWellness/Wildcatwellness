Resources

Channel what you are feeling into positive, meaningful activity. Be informed and proactive around issues that matter to you. Find ways to engage in your community through advocacy in ways that fit for you individually.

It can be challenging to stay motivated when everything around us seems chaotic and scary. But doing something – some action, no matter how small – can lessen some of the uncertainty. What you do does not need to be political – taking a step to stand up for a person or a cause means we are not powerless. Check out the ideas on this page on how to spread kindness in your community.

If you are interested in becoming more politically active, but not sure how or where to start, check out this article for some helpful tips.

Learn Your Strengths

Knowing your strengths can help you address problems more effectively and engage in activities that generate confidence and purpose. Try taking the Character Strengths Survey to identify your strongest traits.

Connect With Your Values

Identifying what is important to us can be one way to foster motivation. Try one of these values card sorts below and then consider how you can express your values on a daily basis.

Vision

What is your vision? Who do you want to be? What do you want to accomplish? These are all great questions to think about, but it is also important to remember that YOU have the power to make these decisions and to affect change in your life. Consider putting together a Vision Board to help create a picture of what you want your life to look like.

Wholeness

Think about what makes you feel whole, grounded, or like you. It can be helpful to consider Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs.
